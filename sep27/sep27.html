Today, I'm going to give you a table of cube numbers from 1-30!

What is a cube number, you ask? Well, a cube number is similar to a square number (i.e. multiplying a number by itself), but it goes one step further by taking the result and multiplying it by the original number. For example, 2 squared is 2 x 2, which is 4, while 2 cubed is 2 x 2 x 2 (or 4 x 2), which is 8.

At least part of this table may be useful (though probably not as much as a table of squares), so pay attention kids!
